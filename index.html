About the Symposium

The Stanford Psychology department will be hosting a one-day symposium to explore the impact of AI on psychological science. The event will feature keynote talks by Emma Brunskill and Michael Bernstein, as well as themed sessions that will explore current and future impacts of AI on theory building, research methodology, and education/training in psychology. There will be ample opportunities for spirited discussion about all of these issues over coffee, lunch, and a reception following the main program.

Keynote Speakers

EB
Keynote
Emma Brunskill
Associate Professor of Computer Science
Stanford University
Emma Brunskill's research focuses on reinforcement learning and machine learning for sequential decision-making, with applications to personalized education and human learning.
MB
Keynote
Michael Bernstein
Professor of Computer Science and Senior Fellow at the Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI
Stanford University
Michael Bernstein's research focuses on the design of social computing systems, crowd-powered AI, and the social dynamics that emerge when people interact through and alongside intelligent systems.
